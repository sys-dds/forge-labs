# Beginner Walkthrough

This clinic solves long-tail content quality and audience fit for Ada by comparing user demand with creator or seller supply before final ranking. Beginners often confuse relevance with ecosystem health: the top raw score can be relevant while also overexposed, risky, too repetitive, too far away, or untrusted.

The broken system did this: post_401, post_101, post_501, post_201 by engagement only, with spam and blocked rows untreated.

The corrected system did this: post_201, post_101, post_501 from quality plus audience fit, with spam rejected and meme downranked.

Rows that prove the lesson: post_101, post_201, post_301, post_401, post_501. A tiny calculation to inspect is `shown / target`: if a creator has 900 shown events against a 300 target, the exposure ratio is 3.0, so the ranker needs a popularity-bias control before filling every slot with the same creator.
