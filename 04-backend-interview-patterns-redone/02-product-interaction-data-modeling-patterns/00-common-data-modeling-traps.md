# Common Data Modeling Traps

Do not confuse a follow with a friendship. Do not show private profile fields to public viewers. Do not count inactive reactions or soft-deleted comments. Do not create a match from one swipe. Do not show a global recent feed when the product asked for followed authors. Do not notify users about their own actions. Do not treat audit events as user-visible activity. Do not recommend already-followed, already-matched, blocked, or reported users. Do not trust read-model counters without source evidence. Do not treat private saves as public content. Do not make hide/report controls global deletes. Do not treat report count as a policy decision. Do not output trust/risk without components. Do not lose appeal reversal or audit lineage. Do not rank marketplace rows by popularity only. Do not hide buyer intent, seller quality, exposure budget, availability, or supply/demand reasons behind one opaque marketplace score. Do not forget that blocks and reports override otherwise valid relationships.
